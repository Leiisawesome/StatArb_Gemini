1. STATISTICAL ARBITRAGE
================================================================================

Academic Foundation: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
-----------------------------------
- Origins: 1980s Morgan Stanley (Gerry Bamberger, Nunzio Tartaglia)
- Theory: Cointegration (Engle-Granger 2003 Nobel Prize)
- Modern Research: Avellaneda & Lee (2010), Gatev et al. (2006)

Key Papers:
- "Statistical Arbitrage in the U.S. Equities Market" (Avellaneda & Lee, 2010)
- "Pairs Trading: Performance of a Relative-Value Arbitrage Rule" (Gatev et al., 2006)
- "A Guide to Statistical Arbitrage" (Chan, 2013)

Institutional Adoption: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Renaissance Technologies (Medallion Fund)
- Two Sigma
- D.E. Shaw
- Citadel's multi-strategy pods

Market Efficiency: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Still profitable but crowded
- Requires: Low latency, high turnover, large universe
- Alpha decay: Moderate (capacity constraints)

Current Relevance (2024): ⭐⭐⭐⭐
✅ ADVANCED - Still core strategy for quant funds
⚠️ Requires: Machine learning for pair selection, intraday execution
📊 Edge: Statistical relationships persist but require sophisticated detection

VERDICT: **ADVANCED** - Nobel Prize foundation, actively researched, requires 
         modern ML for optimal implementation

Current Relevance (2024): ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

================================================================================

Academic Foundation: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
-----------------------------------
- Origins: Market microstructure theory
- Theory: Law of one price, no-arbitrage pricing
- Nobel Prizes: Black-Scholes (1997), Merton (1997)

Key Papers:
- "Arbitrage Theory in Continuous Time" (Merton, 1973)
- "Limits of Arbitrage" (Shleifer & Vishny, 1997)
- "High-Frequency Trading and Price Discovery" (Brogaard et al., 2014)

Institutional Adoption: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **FOUNDATIONAL** - Market makers, HFT firms
- Citadel Securities, Virtu, Jump Trading
- Cross-exchange, cross-asset arbitrage

Market Efficiency: ⭐⭐
- **EXTREMELY CROWDED**
- Pure arbitrage: milliseconds/microseconds
- Requires: Ultra-low latency, co-location
- Alpha decay: Extreme

Current Relevance (2024): ⭐⭐⭐
⚠️ MATURE (for retail/mid-freq) - Edge is in HFT domain
✅ Active Areas:
  - Crypto arbitrage (still inefficient)
  - Cross-border arbitrage
  - ETF arbitrage
  - Options mispricing

VERDICT: **MATURE** - Nobel Prize foundation, but edge requires HFT 
         infrastructure. Retail opportunity in crypto/emerging markets.
